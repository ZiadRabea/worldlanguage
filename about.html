About World Language

Our History

World Language owes its inception to the passion for science and technology nurtured from an early age, particularly by our founder, Ziad Rabea. The journey began with the authoring of a scientific paper at the age of 12, followed by the creation of a social networking website comprising over 70,000 lines of code at the age of 14.

At the age of 16, during a period of personal challenge, the idea for a voice-controlled AI programmer was conceived, motivated not solely by personal needs but also by the desire to provide a tool that could empower those facing adversity and individuals eager to embark on the coding journey.

Concurrently, Ziad recognized the untapped potential for software development in younger family members. However, the complexity of English-centric programming languages posed a formidable barrier. At World Language, our mission is to bridge languages and minds. We believe that the power of coding should be accessible to all, regardless of language or background.
